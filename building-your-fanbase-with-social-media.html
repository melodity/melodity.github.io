Building Your Fanbase With Social Media

May 3rd, 2021 | Keyla Jones

By Keyla Jones


Introduction

Social Media isn’t just a way to kill a few hours, connect with friends and family, it’s also a very powerful way for musicians to engage and make fans. We will be discussing quick and easy tactics to use to grow your fanbase and keep them.


Sections:

  • Getting Started
  • Creating Your Brand
  • How to Make Effective Content
  • Keeping It Real
  • How to Engage With Your Fans
  • Last Notes
  • Other Articles For Building a Fanbase

Getting Started

At this point, you should know why having a fanbase is an important tool for every musician and the free resources available to help you get discovered, their advantages and disadvantages, and have a pretty good idea which you want to go for.


Creating Your Brand

A brand can be anything, it could be your unique personality, the way you dress, the way you talk, the music you make, the message you present. Whether you want one or not, everyone already has a brand and it’s completely unique to who you are. Some artists will deep dive into their unique-ness.


Copyright commons.wikimedia.org

Take a look at Melanie Martinez, she is completely unique, stands out, and is a little over-the-top with everything she does, but that is her brand.



Copyright paradise.london

Whereas, someone like Ed Sheeran seems like, in my opinion, tries to be very down to earth and sticks to his roots. Afterall, he was like a guy with a guitar that played every show he could find. Which is also his brand.

These two are the extreme differences between having a brand, your brand could simply be “a guy with a guitar”, I have seen crazier things.


Deciding on your type of brand
So the first thing to do when starting to create your brand is to think.
1. Is there something really unique about yourself?
2. Do you care deeply about a cause or organization?
3. Is there a certain aesthetic you want to portray?
4. Is there an industry standard around the genre you play? (Example: Rap or Heavy Metal)

If all else fails, look at some of the other artists in your genre and see what they are doing or get ideas about what you like and don’t like and go from there.


Creating a message
As a musician, your message is your music. Unlike companies who have a mission statement, you don’t really need one as a musician, unless you are part of a cause or organization and your music is going to be surrounding that cause.


Your audience as a brand
Another common marketing term/strategy is to determine who your audience is. Afterall, Taylor Swift probably isn’t trying to target 80 year old men with her music. She is going after the younger crowd. Whereas, if you are a country singer, you’re not going to go after the emo, heavy metal crowd. By determining your audience, gives you an idea of where you should start trying to find new fans. This will also help you determine your keywords.


How to Make Effective Content

Content can be anything, videos of your performances, photos of you, your band, your instrument, behind-the-scenes commentary, real talks, memes, even your pet.

Effective Content
The difference between content and effective content all comes down to your fans. Country fans will probably enjoy your flannel photo on the farm compared to the photo of that random heavy metal concert you went to. Catering your content to your fans is key and the only way to find out what you fans like, is to experiment with everything. Have an idea for something to post? Try it and see how many views, likes, and comments you get on it. Then rinse and repeat.


Content Ideas
Depending on the social media platform you’re using, there will be different standards and secrets you’ll want to use.


Facebook
This platform is used for a more friendly, causal appearance. Afterall, people use it to connect with friends and family. This is a great place for a little bit of everything. Photos, casual videos, music videos, originals, memes, long posts, etc. However, this is not the place to spam your post full of hashtags.


Instagram
As a photo platform is all about the visuals. If your photo or video is not something that catches the eye, don’t expect too much attention from it. This is great for photos, videos, funny memes, short lyric posts, etc. Hashtags are the key to getting more views. However, this is not the place for long wordy descriptions, low quality photos or videos.


YouTube
As a video platform, that is what it’s all about. Post your originals, covers, bedroom music, professional lyrics videos, music videos, Vlogs, real talk, behind the scenes, etc. Youtube is all about the hashtags, thoughtful/longer descriptions, and catchy titles.


SoundCloud
As a sound platform, you’ll want to post your more professional sound recordings, with thoughtful tags, short descriptions, and a very eye catching photo.


TikTok
As a 15 second video app, you’ll want to post either funny or the short sweet spot of your songs. You have 15 seconds to catch their attention and that’s what it’s all about. Hashtags are extremely important due to the mass amount of content being posted. However, since so many people use the app, you have a greater chance of being discovered by new fans. This is not the place for long videos, boring stuff, or anything landscape. All videos should be recorded in portrait to do well and don’t forget the #foryourpage.


Custom Website
This is your free space, you can put whatever you want on the website. With this being said, remember that this is the most professional space you have, so make it count. Not the place to share your drunk night photos.


Keeping It Real

Whatever you decide to post, make sure you keep it real. The goal is to get your fans to see you as a real person, someone they can see as a friend. That’s what turns a fan into a loyal fan.


How to Engage With Your Fans

There is no point spending all your time getting fans if you’re not going to engage them and keep their attention. There are a few simple steps that will make a huge difference.

Be Involved
Reply to every comment, even the bad ones. This shows your fans that you care they are taking the time to comment and engage. It makes them more likely to do it again, if they know you are actually reading them.


Kill them with kindness
If you get a negative comment, reply to it but don’t be negative.


Give them opportunities to engage This can be as simple as asking questions, what's their favorite song, what song would they like you to cover, etc. Make games or contests, such as draw our next album cover.


Last Notes

Now that you know how to build your fanbase on social media, next we will talk about attracting new subscribers from different websites.

6 Free Resources To Build Your Fanbase

April 26th, 2021 | Keyla Jones

By Keyla Jones


Introduction

Having an online presence is important for every musician, it allows your fans to connect with you, engage with your content, and keep them up to date with everything new. To get started, below are the basic resources every musician should consider having. We will discuss each of their unique benefits and disadvantages below.


Sections:

  • Facebook
  • Instagram
  • YouTube
  • SoundCloud
  • TikTok
  • Custom Website
  • Last Notes
  • Other Articles For Building a Fanbase

Facebook Page

Facebook is not only a place to watch funny cat videos and catch up with your friends, it’s also a great place to start building your fanbase. It gives you unique opportunities to get discovered by new fans and make connections with other local musicians.
It’s a great place to start building a fanbase by inviting your friends and family to the page. I will say that it is harder to attract new fans on Facebook alone, but since everyone has their own Social Media preferences, it’s an important tool to have available.

Advantages
1. Easy to invite friends and family to ‘like’ you.
2. Able to check in wherever you perform.
3. More visibility when you perform live.
4. Everyone and their grandma has a Facebook account.
5. Shareability means you will be exposed to more friends of friends and older audiences.
6. Great place to connect with other musicians you meet in a professional manner.

Disadvantages
1. Harder to be discovered by completely new people.
2. Requires really engaging content.
3. No chance for “going viral”, which means it’s a slow and steady game.


Instagram

Instagram is all about the visuals, if you’re not ready to pull out your camera, then it’s not for you. This is a broader platform with a bigger chance of getting new fans if you have visually appealing content. It’s all about band photos, short clips, music memes, gig setups, lyric posts, and funny behind-the-scenes shorts.

Advantages
1. Easier to be discovered by new people.
2. Content is simple to make.
3. Easy to capture the attention of the younger crowd.


Disadvantages
1. Hashtags are everything, learn them and use them.
2. If you content is popular it will happen quick, otherwise it will flop.
3. Easy to get fans, but just as easy to lose them.


YouTube

YouTube is an essential to have. When you are trying to get gigs, being able to send over a YouTube video of your music is very important. This is where you’ll post all your live performances, music videos, lyrics videos, and detailed behind-the-scenes work.

Advantages
1. It’s considered professional to have an account, even if you don’t have a lot of views.
2. Great place to keep all your videos.
3. If you get enough views and subscribers, you’ll have a chance to start making money off of it.


Disadvantages
1. It is a snail speed game. It can take years to build your following.
2. Discoverability is very hard to get, due to the mass amount of content being posted.
3. You have to post weekly to build a following.


SoundCloud

SoundCloud is where you should post all your professional audio. It’s a very popular platform that people use to listen to unique covers and original songs.

Advantages
1. Easy to link to your songs.
2. Ability to embed your playlist into websites.
3. Shareability is great.


Disadvantages
1. Hard to get new fans just by posting on their site since there isn’t really “suggested content”.
2. There is a lot of content available by a lot of people.
3. Not really a “fan or follower” set up for this platform.


TikTok

TikTok is no longer just for kids. Everyone is joining in and taking their own spin on it, with a lot of success, quickly. Since there are so many people using this app constantly, you have a very big chance of growing a fanbase quickly. This is the best place for short clips, funny things, choruses of new songs, etc.

Advantages
1. Discoverability is extremely high with the right hashtags.
2. Your content needs to either be funny, really impressive, or on trend.
3. Good lighting is a requirement.


Disadvantages
1. Everything must be taken in portrait mode, landscapes do very poorly on the app.
2. 15 seconds is the sweet spot, not great for long videos.
3. Hashtags are everything, learn them, use them, love them!


Custom Website

Having your own personal website shows that you're serious. It’s a central hub for everything that has to do with your band. With this being said, if you are just starting out, then it’s not going to help you. This is for more professional musicians that are already doing paid gigs.

Advantages
1. Very professional place for your band.
2. Gives your current fanbase a central hub for everything.
3. Gives you the opportunity to make money from ads and merch, if you already have the following.


Disadvantages
1. If you do not already have a following, you will not likely spend a lot of money for little to no outcome.
2. There are start-up costs associated.
3. It is hard to get traffic if you do not have a steady fanbase.


Last Notes

Why You Need a Fanbase

April 19th, 2021 | Keyla Jones

By Keyla Jones


Introduction

A fanbase is one of the most important tools any musician can have. Fans not only support your music, buy your merchandise, and come to your shows, but they can also give you a community. Having a loyal fanbase will help you make money, get bigger gigs, and help you get even more fans.


Sections:

  • 4 Reasons Why You Need a Fanbase
  • Last Notes
  • Other Articles For Building a Fanbase

4 Reasons Why You Need a Fanbase

Building a loyal fanbase is one of the most important skills a musician should have. Afterall, fans are what make or break musicians. Without any fans, you are just some guy with a guitar.


1. Fans Make You Money

From a business standpoint, fans are your customers. They buy your merchandise and go to your shows. Afterall, what’s a business without any customers?


2. Fans Get You Better Gigs

In most cases, how many fans you have determines your popularity as a musician. In bigger venues, it’s very common to ask you what your draw is. Your draw is the amount of fans you have and can bring into your show.
More fans, means more popularity. Afterall, when a large venue has a choice between a musician with 1,000 fans or 100,000 fans, they will most likely go with the latter.


3. Fans Lead to More Fans

In marketing, word of mouth is the most successful technique to use. This is because the information is coming from a reliable source therefore, they are more likely to follow it. Fans are the ambassadors to your brand, they want to share your work with the world, which leads to more fans.


4. Fans Support You Through Thick and Thin

Ever hear the news stories about a famous musician separating from their record label due to differences? Even with them temporarily being label-less, they still manage to be major news and very popular? This is because of their fanbase. Loyal fans are the ones that will stick with you through everything because they love your music and your message.



Last Notes

When I first started studying marketing, there was a saying that could not be more true. It said that there is no point in painting the Mona Lisa, if you’re just going to keep it in your closet. No one is going to know how incredible of a painter you are, no one is going to appreciate your talent, and of course, you are not going to make any money from your skills.
Now that you know the importance of a fanbase, it’s time to start building yours! Next we will be discussing 6 free resources every musician should use for building a fanbase.
